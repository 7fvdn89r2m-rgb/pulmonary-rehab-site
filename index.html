What is pulmonary rehabilitation?

Pulmonary rehabilitation is a programme combining exercise training, breathing strategies, and education to help reduce breathlessness, improve fitness, and support everyday activities.

  • Benefits: better walking tolerance, symptom control, and confidence.
  • Includes: aerobic exercise, strengthening, breathing techniques, pacing, and self-management.
  • Tip: start small and build up gradually.

Safety & when to stop

Stop and seek advice if you have:

  • Chest pain/tightness or new severe breathlessness
  • Dizziness, fainting, or feeling very unwell
  • Blue lips/fingers, confusion, or severe wheeze
  • Severe oxygen drop if you monitor SpO₂ (follow your clinician’s target)

If symptoms are severe or sudden, call emergency services. If you’re unsure, contact your clinical team.

Breathlessness rating (mMRC)

The Modified Medical Research Council (mMRC) scale grades breathlessness in daily life:

  • 0: breathless only with strenuous exercise
  • 1: short of breath when hurrying or walking up a slight hill
  • 2: walk slower than peers or stop after ~15 minutes on level ground
  • 3: stop after ~100 metres or a few minutes on level ground
  • 4: too breathless to leave the house or breathless when dressing

You can note your score at baseline and review changes over time.
